Building Operational Foundations for Scale

When rapid growth outpaces operational maturity

The Problem:

  • Critical knowledge trapped in key employees' heads
  • No documented SOPs or workflows
  • Limited visibility into operational or financial data
  • Systems that work now but will break under growth

What happens when GV drives acquisition without this foundation:

High patient/user volume overwhelms staff → Quality degrades → Operational chaos → Growth marketing ROI suffers because the "bucket has a hole in it"

How Corvo Labs Solves This — Part 1

Phase 1: Discovery (Weeks 1-2)

  • Interview all key stakeholders
  • Map current business workflows end-to-end
  • Begin data consolidation from existing systems
  • Deliverable: Discovery memo, workflow maps, draft SOP list

Phase 2: Documentation & Systems (Weeks 3-5)

  • Create detailed SOPs with follow-up interviews
  • Build operational dashboards (using existing tools)
  • Document cost of goods vs. revenue by service line
  • Deliverable: Complete SOP library, operational dashboards

How Corvo Labs Solves This — Part 2

Phase 3: Roadmap & Enablement (Weeks 6-8)

  • Synthesize into strategic roadmap
  • Build prioritization framework clients can apply independently
  • Identify automation opportunities (non-clinical workflows)
  • Train team for independent maintenance
  • Deliverable: Strategic roadmap, prioritization framework, trained team

Success = clients can scale independently with clear visibility and documented processes

Validating Direction Before Major Investment

When product assumptions need testing before scaling spend

Critical product questions:

  • Is this the right problem to solve?
  • Will users actually engage with this feature?
  • What's the minimum we need to ship to test our hypothesis?
  • Should we invest in building this, or pivot?

What happens when GV drives acquisition without this clarity:

Marketing promotes a product that doesn't resonate → Acquisition spend scales before PMF confirmed → Expensive pivots after significant marketing investment

How Corvo Labs Validates Product Direction — Part 1

Discovery & Hypothesis Formation (Weeks 1-2)

  • Stakeholder interviews + user research
  • Map assumptions and identify riskiest unknowns
  • Define success criteria and validation approach
  • Deliverable: Discovery memo, hypothesis list, validation plan

Rapid Prototyping & Testing (Weeks 3-6)

  • Build lightweight prototypes (Figma, clickable demos, MVPs)
  • Instrument for real usage data
  • Run user testing sessions
  • Deliverable: Tested prototypes, usage data, insights report

How Corvo Labs Validates Product Direction — Part 2

Recommendation & Roadmap (Weeks 7-8)

  • Synthesize findings into decision-grade evidence
  • Recommend: Build, Pivot, or Sunset
  • Provide prioritized feature roadmap (if "Build")
  • Provide alternative directions (if "Pivot" or "Sunset")
  • Deliverable: Executive summary, roadmap, next steps

Success = high-confidence decision with real evidence, not opinions
